# F1 Companion App Landscape (April 2026)

Research conducted during /plan-ceo-review on 2026-04-06.

## Competitive Landscape

### Official

| Product | Type | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|
| F1 Official App | Mobile app | Live timing, team radio, driver tracker, fantasy | Data-heavy, no explanation for casuals |
| F1 Live Timing (web) | Web dashboard | Official data source, real-time | Raw numbers, no context |

### Power User Tools

| Product | Type | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|
| MultiViewer | Desktop app | Detailed timing, onboards, telemetry overlay, mini sectors | Power users only, steep learning curve |
| F1-Dash | Web app | Clean live timing UI, lightweight | No explanation, data-only |
| F1 Replay Timing | Self-hosted | Post-race analysis, granular data | Self-hosted, technical users only |

### Schedule/Widget Apps

| Product | Type | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|
| NextGP | iOS app | Beautiful widgets, countdown, privacy-first | Schedule only, no live companion |
| PlanB F1 | Web app | Race weekend companion | Shallow, no real-time features |

### Browser Extensions

| Product | Type | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|
| F1 Delayed Live Timing | Chrome extension | Syncs timing with TV delay | Timing only, requires F1 timing subscription |
| Timing71 | Chrome extension | Multi-series timing | Generic, not F1-specific |

## The Gap

The market is crowded for data dashboards and schedule widgets. The market is empty for:

1. **Real-time plain-English explanation** for casual/new fans
2. **Voice commentary** alongside the broadcast
3. **Progressive depth** (beginner to advanced) in one product
4. **Strategy explanation** (not just data, but what it means)

Nobody is doing AI-powered race explanation. Nobody is doing voice commentary. This is the gap.

## Closest Analogies (Outside F1)

- **StatCast (MLB):** Made baseball data legible to casual viewers with real-time overlays
- **NFL Next Gen Stats:** Turned raw tracking data into stories and probabilities
- **Second-screen apps (NBA, NFL):** Companion experiences alongside broadcasts

The pattern: **explanation is a product category, not a feature.**

## Key Insight

The existing F1 landscape is bifurcated:
- Too simple (schedule widgets, countdown apps)
- Too complex (MultiViewer, raw timing dashboards)

F1 Companion OS sits in the middle: making F1 understandable in real-time for the millions of fans who came from Drive to Survive but don't understand strategy.
